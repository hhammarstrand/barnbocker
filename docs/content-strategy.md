# Content Strategy — Fifi the Fox Book Series
*Version 1.0 — Creative Director, April 2026*

## Series Vision

The Fifi the Fox series is a **developmentally-anchored digital picture book series** for ages 1–5, delivered on iPhone/iPad. Each book:
- Centers Fifi as the constant, familiar anchor
- Introduces one clear concept or emotional theme per book
- Features at least one side character (Bruno, Bella, Oliver, or new introductions)
- Totals 80–140 words across 6 pages
- Ends with a positive, resolved feeling — no cliffhangers for this age group

---

## Series arc: Year 1 (Books 1–10)

### Book 1 — **Fifi's Friendship Adventure** *(Done)*
**Theme:** Friendship / meeting someone new  
**Featured characters:** Fifi, Bruno  
**Setting:** Forest  
**Core message:** Making a new friend is exciting  
**Target age sweet-spot:** 3–5

---

### Book 2 — **Fifi's Counting Day**
**Theme:** Numbers 1–5  
**Featured characters:** Fifi, Oliver  
**Setting:** Meadow in summer  
**Concept:** Fifi and Oliver find things in the meadow — 1 bee, 2 butterflies, 3 flowers, 4 pebbles, 5 apples — counting together on each page  
**Core message:** Counting is fun, numbers are everywhere  
**Target age sweet-spot:** 2–4  
**Notes:** Simple repetition structure ("One... two... three!") works perfectly for this age; Oliver as guide is natural fit

---

### Book 3 — **Fifi Shares Her Berries**
**Theme:** Sharing  
**Featured characters:** Fifi, Bella  
**Setting:** Forest berry bushes, early autumn  
**Concept:** Fifi finds a big pile of berries. She wants to eat them all, but notices Bella is hungry. She shares — and discovers sharing feels even better  
**Core message:** Sharing makes everyone (including you) feel good  
**Target age sweet-spot:** 2–4  
**Notes:** Classic "I want it all → I share → I feel happy" arc; keeps it emotionally safe and resolved

---

### Book 4 — **Fifi's Bedtime**
**Theme:** Bedtime routine / sleep  
**Featured characters:** Fifi (solo book)  
**Setting:** Fifi's cozy den  
**Concept:** Fifi goes through her bedtime routine step by step — bath, pajamas, brush teeth, story, hug, lights out, dream. Very calm, repetitive structure  
**Core message:** Bedtime is safe and cozy  
**Target age sweet-spot:** 1–3  
**Notes:** Solo book works well here — mirrors the child's own alone-time at night; ideal as a literal bedtime companion

---

### Book 5 — **Fifi Discovers the Seasons**
**Theme:** Nature / seasons  
**Featured characters:** Fifi, Bruno  
**Setting:** The forest across four seasons  
**Concept:** Fifi and Bruno walk through the forest and notice how it changes — spring flowers, summer sun, autumn leaves, winter snow. One page per season + wrap-up  
**Core message:** Nature changes and every season is beautiful  
**Target age sweet-spot:** 3–5  
**Notes:** Great opportunity for rich, varied illustration palette; builds early science observation

---

### Book 6 — **Fifi Learns the ABCs**
**Theme:** Letters / alphabet  
**Featured characters:** Fifi, Oliver  
**Setting:** Oliver's library tree  
**Concept:** Oliver shows Fifi letters on big colorful cards. Each spread focuses on 1–2 letters with a matching animal/object (A is for acorn, B is for butterfly…). Fifi repeats each one excitedly  
**Core message:** Letters are the start of reading  
**Target age sweet-spot:** 3–5  
**Notes:** Can be expanded to a mini-series (ABCs part 1 = A–M, part 2 = N–Z); illustration of each letter with character or object is visually rich

---

### Book 7 — **Fifi Feels Brave**
**Theme:** Emotions / bravery  
**Featured characters:** Fifi, Bella  
**Setting:** A small hill, a stream crossing  
**Concept:** Fifi wants to cross a wobbly bridge over a stream. She feels scared. Bella is already too nervous to try. Fifi takes a deep breath, crosses slowly, and cheers. Bella follows  
**Core message:** Feeling scared is okay — you can try anyway  
**Target age sweet-spot:** 3–5  
**Notes:** Important emotional skill; should visually show the fear as small/manageable, never threatening; ends with mutual celebration

---

### Book 8 — **Fifi's Rainy Day**
**Theme:** Weather / staying home / creativity  
**Featured characters:** Fifi, Bruno  
**Setting:** Fifi's den on a rainy day  
**Concept:** It's raining and Fifi can't go outside. She feels bored. Bruno comes over and they invent indoor games — building a blanket fort, drawing pictures, pretend cooking  
**Core message:** Rainy days can be magical too  
**Target age sweet-spot:** 2–4  
**Notes:** Highly relatable for young children; celebrates imagination; cozy winter/indoor palette

---

### Book 9 — **Fifi Meets Someone New**
**Theme:** New experiences / introductions  
**Featured characters:** Fifi + new character: **Milo the Mouse**  
**Setting:** Near a tree stump  
**Concept:** Fifi finds a tiny mouse (Milo) who is lost. They're different sizes and Milo is nervous. Fifi is patient, gentle, and kind. She helps Milo find his way home  
**Core message:** Being kind to those who are different from you matters  
**Target age sweet-spot:** 3–5  
**Notes:** Introduces a 5th recurring character; size contrast (big/small) is visually engaging for young children; supports early empathy development  
**New character profile — Milo the Mouse:** Tiny brown field mouse, big round ears, bright nervous eyes. Color palette: `#A0785A` (brown), `#F5DEB3` (belly). Timid but sweet; will appear in future books.

---

### Book 10 — **Fifi Says Thank You**
**Theme:** Gratitude / manners  
**Featured characters:** Fifi, all side characters  
**Setting:** A forest clearing — a small celebration  
**Concept:** Fifi realizes her friends have each done something kind for her. She makes a point to visit each one and say thank you in a personal way. The final page is a group hug  
**Core message:** Saying thank you shows you care  
**Target age sweet-spot:** 2–5  
**Notes:** Ensemble cast makes this a great "series milestone" book; revisits all established characters; strong for emotional vocabulary building

## Content Guardrails

- No conflict that isn't resolved within the same book
- No scary animals, strangers, or threatening situations
- No separation from parent/caregiver as a central plot point (too anxiety-inducing for 1–3s)
- No moral that shames the child ("Fifi was BAD") — only growth framing ("Fifi learned…")
- All books end with warmth, safety, connection
